# 3D Printing Suppliers

## What to 3D Print

3D printing is the key differentiator that enables custom charm shapes beyond standard alphabet letters:

- **Custom charms** — letters, symbols, breed silhouettes, AirTag holders — the main product differentiator
- **Collar prototypes** — test sizing, width, and buckle placement before committing to BioThane stock
- **Hardware prototypes** — buckle and hardware form-factor testing
- **Display stands / packaging inserts** — branded retail presentation

---

## Best Materials for Charms

| Material | Properties | Best For |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| **TPU (flexible)** | Soft, stretchy, waterproof, impact-resistant | Slide-on charms that snap over collar strap |
| **Resin (SLA)** | High detail, rigid, smooth surface | Fine lettering, breed silhouettes, decorative charms |
| **Nylon SLS** | Durable, matte finish, no support marks | Small batch production, functional parts |
| **PETG** | Tough, slightly flexible, food-safe options | Hardware prototypes, AirTag holders |

**Recommendation for initial charm prototypes:** TPU (flexible) — most practical for charms that need to slide on and stay on the collar strap without cracking.

## First Step Workflow

1. Design a letter charm in **Tinkercad** — model it as a rectangular block with a horizontal through-hole on each side (left and right) sized to fit the collar strap width
2. Export as `.STL`
3. Send to **3D Creative** or post on **Treatstock** for quotes
4. Print 5–10 test charms in **TPU** — estimated cost €5–15
5. Thread onto a BioThane test collar to validate fit, snap resistance, and look
6. Iterate design if needed before ordering a production batch
